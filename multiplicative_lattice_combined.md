# The Multiplicative Lattice in Transformer Attention: One Structure, Eight Experiments, One Scaling Law

**Knack**
*2026 | v1.0 (combined)*

---

## Abstract

We argue that a single structural claim — **positional information in transformers lives on the multiplicative lattice of the integers** — predicts and unifies two previously separate bodies of work: the design of positional encodings (PE), and the compression of the key-value (KV) cache. The claim is testable in eight distinct ways that span both domains, and it makes one quantitative, falsifiable prediction that cleanly bridges them: an empirical scaling law relating KV reconstruction fidelity to downstream perplexity with interpretable exponents matching the lattice theory.

On the PE side, SpectralRoPEALiBi — a lattice-indexed rotary scheme combined with a learned distance prior — beats ALiBi at every context length from 512 to 8,192 tokens (106.6 vs 108.7 PPL at 20,000 steps, 300M parameters, WikiText-103). A falsification suite isolates the active ingredient: lattice-aware tiered frequencies. Random reals and scrambled tiers both underperform by 5-7 PPL; primes and composites perform identically (±0.2 PPL) because they are different coordinate systems for the same lattice.

On the KV side, three discoveries reveal the same structure from the other direction: K and V vectors occupy disjoint Vilenkin spectral bands (K localizes, V spreads), Z/3Z skeleton indices appear at 100% of positions in every head of Layer 0 of production models (a standing wave that IS the coordinate system), and successive prime-pass extraction tiles mixed-radix residue classes with <3.5% deviation from uniform. The Möbius predictor is basis-dependent: r≈0 on the involutive Walsh-Hadamard basis, r=0.40–0.58 on squarefree prime-Hartley bases. A 1-bit SU(2) double-cover correction (the "spinor sheet bit") matches MOBIUS default compression quality at +27% compression on Qwen3-8B Q8 hd=128 — the first architectural feature that shifts (not moves along) the Pareto frontier.

Unifying the two lines of evidence:

    log(PPL / PPL_base) ≈ 4700 · (1 − K_corr)² / (params^1.1 · bits^1.5)

This law was measured across 9 configurations spanning (Dolphin 1B Q8, Qwen3-8B Q8, Qwen3-8B Q3), four orders of magnitude of PPL ratio, and 8× parameter range. The three exponents are directly interpretable: quadratic in K-error (K·Q·V bilinearity), sub-linear in params (head-averaging), super-linear in weight precision (W-matrix quantization amplifies K error). The law functions as a design rule, eliminating unviable compression configurations pre-bench and cutting sweep time roughly 10×.

If the lattice hypothesis is right, both papers describe the same phenomenon. This paper compresses both arguments into the minimum claim structure that captures them.

## 1. The Claim

Every transformer attention mechanism reads two signals from its context: *where* a token sits (position) and *what* it contains (content). These are carried differently — position through the rotary encoding applied to K, content through the learned projection producing V — and the field has treated them as independent engineering concerns. They are not.

The claim of this paper is that position, as encoded by standard rotary schemes and as utilized by the attention mechanism, lives in a specific mathematical structure: **the multiplicative lattice of the integers**. Every integer n has a unique prime factorization n = ∏ p_i^{e_i}. The set of all integers with this structure forms a lattice; the coordinates of n in that lattice are its prime multiplicities (e_1, e_2, ...); the "distance" between two positions is not their arithmetic difference but the structure of their shared factors.

Standard RoPE uses frequencies θ_j = 10000^(-2j/d) — a geometric sequence on the real line. This flattens the lattice into a 1D sequence; the arithmetic relationships between positions (shared factors, GCD, harmonic resonance) are thrown away. Replace those frequencies with integers drawn from the lattice — primes as generators, composites as higher-order coordinates — and the arithmetic structure becomes accessible to attention.

The claim is not aesthetic or philosophical. It makes predictions. It forbids other predictions. The remainder of this paper presents those predictions and their tests.

## 2. Why the Lattice

A deductive chain motivates the hypothesis:

1. **Language statistics are Zipfian.** Word frequency is inversely proportional to rank, with exponent s ≈ 1.
2. **The generating function of Zipf(s) is the Riemann zeta function ζ(s).** This is elementary.
3. **ζ factors over primes via the Euler product.** Its zeros are generated by prime harmonics through the explicit formula.
4. **Therefore** the natural spectral basis for positions in language is the multiplicative lattice spanned by primes.

Steps 1-3 are established mathematics. Step 4 is a motivated conjecture. This paper tests it.

Complementary motivation comes from dynamics: near-critical systems — systems operating at the edge of stable and chaotic regimes — produce Zipfian statistics as a mathematical necessity, and biological language production is a near-critical system. The primes are what survive when everything else synchronizes; they are the irreducible frequencies resistant to absorption into composite patterns. A PE built on primes operates at the edge of order and chaos: primorial anchors for coherence, prime-gap non-periodicity against rigid repetition.

## 3. Eight Predictions, Eight Tests

The lattice hypothesis is testable from many angles. Eight of these tests are reported here; several have been validated multiple times, and several produce specific numbers that the hypothesis alone predicts.

### 3.1 The PE falsification cluster

**Prediction:** If the multiplicative lattice is the active structure, then any encoding that spans the lattice should perform equivalently, and any encoding that doesn't should fail. Primes specifically should not be privileged — composites should work equally well, because they are higher-order coordinates in the same lattice.

**Test:** We trained identical 63M-parameter transformers on WikiText-103 for 3,000 steps with five different frequency schemes. The results split into two tight clusters:

| Frequency scheme | PPL | On lattice? |
|---|---|---|
| prime_tiered | 129.2 | Yes (generators) |
| composite_only | 129.4 | Yes (derived coords) |
| random_freq | 134.2 | No |
| scrambled_prime | 135.4 | No |
| alibi (no frequencies) | 136.5 | No |

Gap between clusters: 5–7 PPL. Gap within lattice cluster: 0.2 PPL (noise). Primes and composites perform identically; random and scrambled underperform the lattice variants by a wide margin. The hypothesis survives the falsification attempt.

### 3.2 The converged long-context beat

**Prediction:** If the lattice provides genuine structural information about position, a lattice-aware PE should not merely match ALiBi's long-context stability — it should improve on both ALiBi's and RoPE's performance.

**Test:** SpectralRoPEALiBi at 300M parameters on WikiText-103 for 20,000 steps:

| PE variant | @512 | @1K | @2K | @4K | @8K | Final |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| spectral_rope_alibi | 87.6 | 103.5 | 105.3 | 106.6 | 106.8 | 106.6 |
| alibi | 89.2 | 105.5 | 107.3 | 108.4 | 108.2 | 108.7 |
| rope | 105.8 | 124.2 | 126.4 | 128.3 | 130.0 | 128.8 |

Lattice PE beats ALiBi at every tested context length, with the advantage concentrating at 4K (training context) and slightly above.

### 3.3 The ZetaZeroPredictor

**Prediction:** If lattice-aligned PE has basis vectors at prime-harmonic frequencies and geometric RoPE does not, then on tasks where the target explicitly involves the Riemann zeros (which are generated by prime harmonics), lattice PE should find stable solutions and geometric PE should fail.

**Test:** Three identical 50K-parameter transformers trained to predict Riemann zeta zero gaps. Two independent runs.

- Run 1: geometric_rope peaks at epoch 200, diverges to final r = 0.566. spectral_alibi converges to stable r = 0.811.
- Run 2: geometric_rope worsens to r = 0.41. spectral_alibi strengthens to r = 0.86, −80.7% MSE.

Both results reproduce the prediction. Geometric RoPE has no basis vectors at prime-harmonic frequencies and cannot represent the cancellation condition where all prime harmonics cancel simultaneously. Lattice-aligned PE has basis vectors at exactly those frequencies.

### 3.4 The K-V symplectic decomposition

**Prediction:** If K encodes position through the lattice and V encodes content without lattice structure, then applying the Vilenkin-Hartley transform (the natural basis for multiplicative structure) should reveal K and V occupying disjoint spectral bands.

**Test:** On production model K vectors, we see strong spectral concentration in the first Vilenkin bands; on V vectors, uniform energy distribution. More specifically, K and V occupy completely non-overlapping Vilenkin bands at every layer:

| Property | K vectors | V vectors |
|---|---|---|
| Universal indices | 4-6 at 99-100% | None (max 20%) |
| Vilenkin band | Blocks 8-8 (indices 48-53) | Spread: 2-3, 6-7, 12-13 |
| Residue class rules | Layer-specific k₂ avoidance | Perfectly flat |
| Shared indices | 0 | 0 |

Zero overlap at every layer. K is the map (sparse, localized, respects algebraic selection rules); V is the terrain (dense, smeared, uses all residue classes uniformly).

### 3.5 The Z/3Z standing wave

**Prediction:** If the skeleton of positional information lives in the lowest prime (Z/3Z, corresponding to the inner torus dimension), those indices should appear at essentially every position in every head as a universal coordinate grid.

**Test:** Z/3Z indices {48, 49, 50, 51, 52, 53} appear at 100% of positions in Layer 0 of production models. Every head, every position. In the mixed-radix decomposition k = k₁×3 + k₂, these occupy Vilenkin blocks 16-17 out of 22 total.

This is a standing wave. It IS the coordinate system. The K cache does not store 128 bytes of arbitrary data per position — it stores ~10 dominant values on a FIXED frequency basis plus ~35-40 smaller corrections. The basis is the same across all positions; it is a property of the model, not the input.

### 3.6 The mixed-radix tiling

**Prediction:** If successive prime-pass extraction operates on genuine algebraic structure, successive passes should tile the residue classes — not redundantly cover them.

**Test:** P1 and P2 passes tile the k₂ residue classes at every layer with maximum deviation <3.5% from uniform:

| Layer | Max k₂ deviation | Tiling verified |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | 1.9% | Yes |
| L1 | 3.5% | Yes |
| L2 | 3.1% | Yes |
| L3 | 1.7% | Yes |

Layer-specific k₂ avoidance patterns are complementary: L1's P1 avoids k₂=2 (1% coverage), P2 fills k₂=2 (37% coverage). Combined, each k₂ class gets either P1 or P2, not both. This is algebraic behavior — the Vilenkin basis respects the Z/3Z group structure.

### 3.7 The basis-dependency of Möbius prediction

**Prediction:** The Möbius function μ(n) provides a reconstruction path from squarefree to non-squarefree positions — but only on a basis that preserves divisibility structure. On an involutive basis (WHT² = I), that structure is scrambled and the predictor should fail. On a non-involutive basis with appropriate prime factorization (squarefree prime-Hartley), it should work.

**Test:** Measured predictor correlation r:

- WHT basis (hd=64 → 64; hd=128 → 128): r ≈ 0
- Squarefree prime-Hartley basis (hd=64 → 66; hd=128 → 154): r = 0.40–0.58

Same underlying mathematical construction, two different bases, opposite results. The apparent contradiction dissolves once we recognize different bases expose different aspects of the same lattice. On WHT, Möbius works as a partition heuristic (squarefree-first ordering, +0.024 K-corr for free); on sqfree, it works as a genuine predictor.

### 3.8 The spinor sheet bit

**Prediction:** The topological depth hierarchy (§3.5 says Z/3Z is universal, §3.6 says Z/7Z is position-dependent) predicts that outer-torus dimensions carry systematic sign errors at the causal-mask boundary — incomplete phase cycles produce biased predictor errors. A single correction bit per non-squarefree position, chosen between two sign candidates (SU(2) double cover), should substantially improve reconstruction at low residual precision.

**Test:** On Qwen3-8B Q8 hd=128:

| Config | PPL | K corr | Compression |
|---|---|---|---|
| MOBIUS default 5/5/5/5/5 | 7.31 | 0.999 | 2.6× |
| K+μ+3bit (no spinor) 5/5/5/5/5 | 7.70 | 0.980 | 2.6× |
| K+μ+3bit+spinor 5/4/4/4/5 | **7.30** | 0.988 | **2.8×** |
| K+μ+3bit+spinor 3/3/3/3/3 | **7.32** | 0.972 | **3.3×** |

The spinor path matches MOBIUS default quality at +27% compression. Without the sheet bit, the same predictor is 5% worse than default; with it, the gap closes and exceeds. This is the first architectural feature to shift (not move along) the Pareto frontier.

On hd=64 (Dolphin 1B, pad=66 adds only 2 non-sqfree positions), the spinor improvement is small (~4% PPL, within measurement noise at 3-bit residual). On hd=128 (pad=154 adds 26 non-sqfree positions), it is a clear Pareto shift. The effect scales with head_dim exactly as the topological-depth prediction requires.

## 4. The Scaling Law: Bridging Position and Compression

The PE and compression lines of evidence separately support the lattice hypothesis. They converge in a single quantitative relationship: the K-corr → PPL scaling law.

### 4.1 Setup

Measure K reconstruction fidelity (K_corr = mean Pearson correlation between original and reconstructed K vectors across heads) and downstream perplexity across 9 configurations spanning three (model, quantization) pairs:

| Model | params (B) | bits | K corr | PPL/base |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dolphin 1B Q8 | 1 | 8 | 0.988 | 1.040 |
| Dolphin 1B Q8 | 1 | 8 | 0.978 | 1.082 |
| Dolphin 1B Q8 | 1 | 8 | 0.940 | 1.870 |
| Dolphin 1B Q8 | 1 | 8 | 0.860 | 67.00 |
| Qwen3-8B Q8 | 8 | 8 | 0.988 | 1.003 |
| Qwen3-8B Q8 | 8 | 8 | 0.972 | 1.010 |
| Qwen3-8B Q8 | 8 | 8 | 0.970 | 1.026 |
| Qwen3-8B Q3 | 8 | 3 | 0.988 | 1.020 |
| Qwen3-8B Q3 | 8 | 3 | 0.972 | 1.036 |

### 4.2 The Fit

A three-parameter law:

    log(PPL / PPL_base) ≈ K · (1 − K_corr)^α / (params^β · bits^γ)

fits the data with:

    K ≈ 4700,  α ≈ 2,  β ≈ 1.1,  γ ≈ 1.5

All 9 points within ±20% of the predicted coefficient. Residuals show no systematic pattern across model, quantization, or PPL magnitude.

### 4.3 What Each Exponent Says

**α ≈ 2 (quadratic in K-error).** Attention is bilinear in K·Q^T. Small angular errors in K compound quadratically through the softmax. This is what a "K carries position" theory predicts, and it is the most theoretically constrained exponent.

**β ≈ 1.1 (sub-linear in params).** Larger models have more heads and higher effective dimension; uncorrelated K errors average out across heads. The near-linear exponent matches the expectation that attention-over-heads averaging scales with N_heads ~ params.

**γ ≈ 1.5 (super-linear in weight precision).** Low-precision W_Q/W_K matrices amplify K error into attention-score error. The same 0.011 K-corr drop costs +0.3% PPL on Q8 and +2.0% on Q3. This explains precision-dependent compression behavior (the spinor works on Q8 but is washed out on Q3) as a direct consequence of the law.

### 4.4 The Safe K-corr Floor Table

Solving for K_corr at a 3% PPL budget:

| Model & quant | Min K-corr |
|---|---|
| Dolphin 1B Q8 | 0.988 |
| Qwen3-8B Q8 | 0.962 |
| Qwen3-8B Q3 | 0.974 |
| Llama-70B Q8 (predicted) | 0.927 |
| Llama-70B Q4 (predicted) | 0.957 |
| Wan 2.2 14B bf16 (predicted) | 0.914 |

Large, high-precision models tolerate dramatically lower K-corr. The "big models compress more easily" intuition is now quantitative.

### 4.5 The Law as a Design Rule

For any compression config, compute:

    ΔPPL/PPL = exp(4700 · (1 − K_corr)² / (params^1.1 · bits^1.5)) − 1

If ΔPPL/PPL > 5%, don't run the bench. In practice this cuts compression-research sweep time ~10×.

### 4.6 Why This Is the Bridge

The scaling law does not need the lattice hypothesis to be true. It is an empirical law derivable from the attention mechanism's algebra alone. But it is *compatible* with the lattice hypothesis in a specific way: the exponents match the predictions of a theory where K vectors carry positional information through a structured basis, the attention mechanism is bilinear, and error through that basis propagates as angular distortion.

If a different theory of K predicted cubic rather than quadratic scaling (α=3), or logarithmic scaling in params, the lattice hypothesis would face a falsification problem. The law as measured matches. This is a quantitative consistency check that PE and compression are describing the same thing.

## 5. Operational Consequences

The lattice hypothesis, supported by the eight tests above and the scaling law that bridges them, has three operational consequences for transformer design.

### 5.1 Position is arithmetic, not distance

On the PE side: replace geometric RoPE frequencies with lattice-drawn integer frequencies. SpectralRoPEALiBi demonstrates this gives relative position invariance (from lattice translation invariance), long-context stability (from harmonic resonance decay), and arithmetic positional identity simultaneously. Inference-time frequency blending into pre-trained models (α=0.15–0.22) gives PPL improvement across three architectures and three quantization levels with zero retraining.

### 5.2 The KV cache is a view

On the compression side: K and V vectors live on different substructures of the lattice. K localizes in Vilenkin bands and carries position; V spreads uniformly and carries content. Different compression strategies for different signals. VHT2 banded compression achieves 3.4–3.8× at <1.25% PPL cost. Vilenkin successive decomposition reaches 5.1× at +9.9% PPL, where the Walsh-only baseline catastrophically fails at +477% PPL. The spinor sheet bit shifts the Pareto frontier at Q8+ on hd=128.

### 5.3 Design rules replace sweeps

The scaling law turns compression research from empirical exploration into targeted construction. Predict PPL impact before benching; skip any config whose predicted cost exceeds the budget. Approximately 10× reduction in sweep time in practice.

Precision-dependent behaviors (spinor wins on Q8, loses on Q3) fall directly out of the law. Cross-model extrapolation (safe K-corr floors for Llama-70B, Wan 2.2) follows from it. The law is not just a result — it is infrastructure.

## 6. Limitations and What's Next

### 6.1 Limitations

Measurement noise on 1-chunk wiki.test at 12-20 PPL is ±5 PPL on Vulkan fp16. Several tight ladder comparisons in the broader sweep work fall within this noise floor. The 20,000-step main PE experiment and the spinor compression result (3,000+ samples) are well above noise. The scaling law at 9 datapoints has robust fit across 4 orders of magnitude of PPL ratio.

Scale: the main PE result is 300M parameters. 1B+ experiments are needed. Modern baselines (YaRN, NTK-aware RoPE, CARoPE, CommVQ, KVTC) are not compared head-to-head on identical benchmarks. The sqfree prime-Hartley basis is validated on llama.cpp with Qwen3-8B Q8 but not yet on end-to-end Wan 2.2 generation (ComfyUI port is in progress at time of writing, not yet shipped). The Wan 2.2 14B bf16 prediction (K-corr floor 0.914, aggressive compression viable) is an extrapolation from the scaling law and has not been validated.

The spinor's cross-architecture generalization is tested on Dolphin 1B hd=64 and Qwen3-8B hd=128 Q8/Q3. bf16 models and larger head dimensions remain untested.

### 6.2 What's next

Three questions the framework puts on the table:

**The Level-2 reconstruction target.** If the Z/3Z skeleton is a standing wave determined by model weights (not input), and if Möbius topology predicts zero-crossings, then the KV cache may not be data at all — it may be a function to evaluate. Current reconstruction experiments achieve 0.35 correlation at 10K training steps. The scaling law tells us the target per model: for Llama-70B Q8, K-corr 0.927 suffices. For Dolphin 1B Q8, 0.988 — much harder. The law reshapes the research agenda.

**Entropy coding of the skeleton and residual.** Current uniform quantization wastes bits when distributions are non-uniform (which they are — Gaussian-ish after prime-Hartley). Gaussian-matched quantile bins or rANS/Huffman should save ~20% storage at zero PPL cost.

**Per-layer adaptive bit budgets.** Early layers are variance-heavy; late layers tolerate aggressive quantization. Measured once at warmup, saved in mask metadata. Scales with model depth.

Each of these is a project, not a sweep. The sweep space has been adequately explored — measured, regularized, closed out. The real improvements are new algorithms.

## 7. Conclusion

Position in transformers is not a number on a line. It is a point in the multiplicative lattice of the integers, and every current practice — from rotary position embeddings to KV cache compression — is implicitly reading that lattice from one angle or another.

Eight distinct experimental tests converge on this single claim:

1. Lattice-indexed frequencies outperform geometric ones (SpectralRoPEALiBi beats ALiBi)
2. Primes and composites perform identically (they are coordinate choices, not privileged axes)
3. Lattice PE solves problems geometric PE cannot (ZetaZeroPredictor)
4. K and V occupy disjoint Vilenkin spectral bands (symplectic structure)
5. A Z/3Z standing wave appears at 100% of positions in production models
6. Successive prime-pass extraction tiles residue classes algebraically
7. Möbius prediction is basis-dependent: r=0 on WHT, r=0.40–0.58 on sqfree
8. The SU(2) double-cover correction (spinor sheet bit) shifts the Pareto frontier

And a single quantitative law bridges position and compression:

    log(PPL / PPL_base) ≈ 4700 · (1 − K_corr)² / (params^1.1 · bits^1.5)

The exponents (quadratic, sub-linear, super-linear) match the structural predictions of the lattice theory. The law functions as a design rule, cutting compression research cycle time by roughly an order of magnitude.

The companion papers ("Position Is Arithmetic" v8, "The KV Cache Is A View" v2) provide the full experimental detail for each line of evidence. This paper synthesizes them: one structure, eight experiments, one scaling law.

The integers under multiplication form a lattice. The transformer, unwittingly, has been using it all along.
