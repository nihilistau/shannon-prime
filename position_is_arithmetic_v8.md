## Abstract

We show that the apparent tradeoff between RoPE-style relative position invariance and ALiBi-style long-context stability is an artifact of encoding position as distance on a number line. When position is instead encoded as a point in the multiplicative lattice of the integers, both properties emerge simultaneously. SpectralRoPEALiBi achieves 106.6 PPL vs ALiBi's 108.7 in a fully converged 20,000-step experiment (300M params, WikiText-103, 4K context), beating ALiBi at every context length from 512 to 8,192 tokens.

The key insight is not that primes specifically are the right frequencies, but that the multiplicative structure of the integers is the natural spectral basis for positional encoding. We demonstrate this through falsification: prime-tiered (129.2 PPL) and composite-tiered (129.4 PPL) frequencies perform identically — because composites are coordinates in the same lattice primes generate. Both dramatically outperform random frequencies (+5.0 PPL), scrambled tier assignment (+6.3 PPL), and pure ALiBi (+7.3 PPL). The active ingredient is lattice-aware, tiered frequency selection — not primality.

We further validate through ZetaZeroPredictor: three identical transformers trained to predict Riemann zeta zero gaps show geometric RoPE diverging (r=0.57) while lattice-aligned PE locks into a stable attractor (r=0.86, −80.7% MSE). We provide a geometric formalization: k prime frequencies × 2 dimensions per RoPE pair define a k-torus embedded in 2k-dimensional real phase space, with empirically measured topological depth — Z/3Z indices appear at 100% of positions while Z/7Z appears at only 35–44%.

Production validation: blending 15–22% composite-tiered frequencies into Llama 3.2 1B via llama.cpp improves perplexity at inference time with zero retraining (PPL −0.82% at Q8_0, −0.66% at Q6_K, −0.61% at Q4_K_M), confirmed across three model architectures. The improvement is universal, robust to quantization level, and structurally explained: prime frequencies correct positional frequency mismatch, a different error from what weight quantization introduces.

An empirical scaling law connecting KV-cache reconstruction fidelity to downstream perplexity provides independent confirmation that K vectors carry positional information through the arithmetic lattice: the quadratic dependence on K-corr is exactly the K·Q·V bilinearity predicted by the theory.

## 1. Introduction

Positional encoding provides transformer models with token order information. Two approaches dominate: RoPE encodes position through frequency-based rotations preserving relative position invariance, and ALiBi replaces frequencies with a linear distance penalty providing long-context stability. The field has treated these properties as fundamentally in tension.

We show this tension is false. It arises from a shared, unexamined assumption: that position is a location on a number line and the meaningful relationship between positions is distance. We replace this with a mathematically grounded alternative: position is a point in the multiplicative lattice of the integers, and the meaningful relationships between positions are their arithmetic structure — shared factors, GCD, harmonic resonance.

### 1.1 The Lattice Hypothesis

The integers under multiplication form a lattice where every number occupies a unique point defined by its prime factorisation. Geometric PE (sinusoidal, RoPE) projects this lattice onto a line — position equals distance — discarding the multiplicative structure. We propose restoring it.

The motivation follows from a deductive chain. Language word frequency follows Zipf's law with s approximately 1. The generating function of Zipf is the Riemann zeta function. The zeta zeros — where the function is maximally informative — are generated by prime harmonics via the explicit formula. Therefore the prime harmonic structure, and the multiplicative lattice it generates, provides a natural spectral basis for encoding positions in language.

### 1.2 Primes as Generators, Composites as Coordinates

Primes are the generators (basis vectors) of the multiplicative lattice. They are analogous to the 1D line segment in the progression from line to circle to sphere to hypersphere. The composite 12 = 2² × 3 is not an alternative to primes — it is a coordinate in the lattice spanned by the prime axes, at position (2,1,0,0,...) in the (p₂, p₃, p₅, p₇,...) basis.

Using 2π/12 as a frequency encodes a harmonic that resonates at multiples of 12 — simultaneously hitting every multiple of 2, 3, 4, and 6. The composite frequency carries its prime factors with it. This is why composite-only frequencies perform identically to prime frequencies: they encode the same lattice through higher-order products rather than generators.

### 1.3 Contributions

We make four contributions:

1. **Architectural.** SpectralRoPEALiBi combines tiered prime-indexed rotary frequencies with a learned ALiBi distance prior, dissolving the RoPE/ALiBi tradeoff.

2. **Experimental.** A falsification suite demonstrates that the active ingredient is lattice-aware tiered frequency selection, not primality. ZetaZeroPredictor provides mechanistic validation across two independent runs. An empirical scaling law on KV-cache reconstruction provides independent support for the K-carries-position claim.

3. **Geometric.** We formalize the phase space as a k-torus with empirically measured topological depth, connecting the stability hierarchy of Vilenkin group components to the causal mask boundary.

4. **Production.** Inference-time frequency injection across three architectures and three quantization levels confirms universal PPL improvement at zero retraining cost.

## 2. Background

### 2.1 The Zipf–Zeta Connection

Natural language exhibits Zipfian statistics: word frequency is inversely proportional to rank. This is a mathematical consequence of near-critical dynamics in systems operating at the edge of order and chaos. The most common words — I, me, you, us — are short because Shannon optimisation favours brevity for high-frequency signals. The generating function of the Zipf distribution is the Riemann zeta function. The zeta function factors over primes via the Euler product. Therefore, prime harmonic structure is the natural coordinate system of language.

### 2.2 The Knight's Move: From Lines to Lattices

In the progression from 1D to nD geometry, each dimension is not stacked — it is integrated. The surface area of an n-sphere is the derivative of the volume. The Archimedean insight is that the sphere's cross-section varies as you traverse the new axis (x² + y² = 1 − z²), and the volume cannot be computed by naive multiplication.

The multiplicative lattice has the same structure. The resonance function R(Δ) = Σ cos(2π·Δ/p) / p does not decompose into independent per-prime contributions at composite distances — because the harmonics interfere. A primorial distance Δ = 30 = 2×3×5 achieves maximum constructive interference; a prime distance Δ = 17 achieves destructive interference because it is coprime to all small primes.

### 2.3 The 5-Torus: Geometric Formalization

Using k primes to define a Vilenkin basis, each prime p represents an independent circular S¹ dimension. Transformers via RoPE operate on (cos, sin) pairs, meaning each prime frequency is a rotation in a 2-dimensional real plane. Therefore k primes × 2 dimensions per plane = a 2k-dimensional real phase space. The manifold is a k-torus T^k, isomorphic to (S¹)^k, embedded in 2k-D.

For k = 5 primes (2, 3, 5, 7, 11): a 5-torus in 10D. This is the geometric object that the positional encoding defines. The "knight's move" — the Cartesian product construction from n-ball theory — is how each dimension is built from the previous.

Empirical measurement reveals topological depth: the inner dimensions (Z/2Z, Z/3Z) are robust to causal masking while the outer dimensions (Z/7Z, Z/11Z) are fragile. Z/3Z indices appear at 100% of positions in Layer 0 of a production model — every head, every position. Z/7Z indices appear at only 35–44% of positions. This gradient is a measurable property of the manifold, not an engineering choice.

### 2.4 Why Geometric PE Is Structurally Blind

RoPE uses frequencies θ_j = 10000^(−2j/d). This geometric sequence creates redundant coverage at some scales and gaps at others. There is no mechanism for encoding the arithmetic relationships between positions. Lattice PE encodes that 12 = 2²×3 shares factors with 6 = 2×3 in a way that 13 (prime, coprime to both) does not.

## 3. Method

### 3.1 SpectralRoPEAttention

We replace geometric RoPE frequencies with integer-indexed frequencies allocated across attention heads in three tiers:

| Tier | Heads (n=12) | Range | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local | 0–2 (25%) | 2..101 | Word/syntax |
| Mid | 3–6 (33%) | 101..1009 | Clause/paragraph |
| Long | 7–11 (42%) | 1009..8209 | Section/document |

Critical negative result: replacing ALL frequencies uniformly with zeta zeros caused +370% context degradation. Per-head scale diversity is load-bearing. Tiered allocation preserves diversity while introducing arithmetic structure.

### 3.2 SpectralALiBiAttention — The Primary Architecture

Prime rotations combined with a learned ALiBi distance prior:

    score(i,j) = α_h · R_rotate(i,j) − slope_h · |i−j| + β_h · QK(i,j)/√d

ALiBi slopes initialised to standard values and made learnable. A per-head freq_scale parameter (init=1.0) allows the model to discover its natural harmonic basis from data.

This architecture simultaneously achieves relative position invariance (from arithmetic relationships, which are translation-invariant in the lattice), long-context stability (from harmonic resonance decay), and arithmetic positional identity (from the lattice structure itself).

### 3.3 HarmonicStringAttention

The attention score is derived directly from prime harmonic interference:

    R(Δ) = [Σ cos(2π·Δ/p) / p] / R(0)

R(Δ) has a physical interpretation: the amplitude of constructive interference between prime harmonic waves at distance Δ. Primorials achieve R = 0.58–0.70 (maximum constructive interference); prime distances achieve R = −0.11 to −0.47 (destructive interference). This is the edge of chaos in an attention mechanism.

## 4. Experiments

### 4.1 Setup

Main run: 300M parameters (24L, d=768, 12H, FFN=3072). WikiText-103. 4096-token context, 20,000 steps. Falsification runs: 63M parameters on RunPod Blackwell (3,000 steps, 4K context). ZetaZeroPredictor: 50K-parameter transformers (3L, d=64, 4H) for 10,000 epochs. Production alpha sweep: Dolphin 3.0 Llama 3.2 1B at three quantization levels.

SpectralRoPEALiBi crosses below ALiBi at approximately step 1,500 and maintains a stable 2.1 PPL advantage to convergence.

At 16K both SRA and ALiBi converge — the arithmetic advantage concentrates at training context and moderate extrapolation where position-pair relationships are statistically meaningful.

### 4.3 The Falsification Suite

Eight control variants isolate whether prime structure specifically or the multiplicative lattice is the active ingredient:

| Variant | PPL | Δ vs prime_tiered | Lattice? |
|---|---|---|---|
| prime_tiered | 129.2 | — | Yes (generators) |

The results split cleanly. Lattice-aware variants (~129) are statistically indistinguishable. Lattice-unaware variants (~134–137) form a second, worse cluster. The gap between clusters is 5–7 PPL; within the lattice cluster, 0.2 PPL (noise).

Why composites work: 12 = 2²×3 at frequency 2π/12 resonates at multiples of 12, simultaneously hitting multiples of 2, 3, 4, and 6. The composite inherits its prime factors' arithmetic structure. Using composites is like computing a 3-sphere's volume from its surface area rather than the generating radius — a different entry point into the same structure.

Why random fails: random reals are off-lattice. No multiplicative relationships. No knight's move possible because there is no grid.

Why scrambled fails: correct frequencies at wrong scale tiers. Like having the correct n-ball formula but computing a 3-sphere using the 7-sphere's surface area. Dimensional assignment is load-bearing.

### 4.4 ZetaZeroPredictor — Mechanistic Validation

Three identical 50K-parameter transformers trained to predict Riemann zeta zero gaps from a 50-gap context window.

Run 1: geometric_rope peaks at epoch 200 then diverges to final r = 0.566. spectral_alibi finds stable attractor at r = 0.811.

Run 2 (independent): geometric worsens to r = 0.41. spectral_alibi strengthens to r = 0.86, −80.7% MSE.

Results reproduce and strengthen on replication. Geometric RoPE finds no stable solution because it has no basis vectors at prime harmonic frequencies — it cannot represent the cancellation condition where all prime harmonics cancel simultaneously. Lattice-aligned PE has basis vectors at exactly those frequencies.

## 5. Theoretical Analysis

### 5.1 The Deductive Argument

(1) Language obeys Zipf(s≈1). (2) The generating function of Zipf is ζ(s). (3) The zeta zeros encode the prime harmonic structure of ζ. (4) Therefore the multiplicative lattice generated by primes provides a natural spectral basis for language positions. Steps (1)–(3) are established mathematics. Step (4) is a motivated conjecture supported by the experimental evidence.

### 5.2 Near-Criticality and Language

The brain operates near, but not exactly at, the critical point between stable and chaotic dynamics. This sweet spot enhances computational flexibility and sensitivity to inputs. Near-critical systems produce Zipfian statistics as a mathematical necessity. Language, as the output of a near-critical system, inherits this structure. The primes are what survive when everything else synchronises — the irreducible frequencies that resist absorption into composite patterns. A PE built on primes operates at the edge of order and chaos: primorial anchors for coherence, prime-gap non-periodicity against rigid repetition.

### 5.3 The Dimensional Analogy

The relationship between primes and composites in the multiplicative lattice mirrors the n-ball progression precisely:

| n-ball | Multiplicative lattice |
|---|---|
| 1D line (2r) — generator | Primes — generators |
| 2D circle — integral of swept line | Semiprimes (6=2×3) — 2-factor products |
| 3D sphere — integral of swept circle | 3-factor composites (30=2×3×5) |
| nD ball — recursive integration | Primorials — maximal resonance |

Just as V₃ cannot be computed by naively multiplying V₂ × 2r (because the circle's radius depends on z), a composite's resonance cannot be decomposed into independent prime channels. Archimedes' theorem (sphere surface area = cylinder surface area) is the lossless projection: the dependence is already encoded in the structure.

### 5.4 The Topological Depth Hierarchy

The 5-torus formalization (§2.3) predicts a stability gradient. Inner torus dimensions corresponding to small primes have long wavelengths and survive the causal mask; outer dimensions corresponding to larger primes have fine resolution and are fragile. Empirical measurement on production model K vectors confirms this:

| Group | Universality | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Z/3Z | 100% all positions, all heads | Standing wave — the skeleton |
| Z/5Z | 70–97% | Semi-crystalline — the detail |
| Z/7Z | 35–44% | Position-dependent — the texture |

This gradient maps onto the Prime Number Theorem error term: the skeleton corresponds to Li(x) (the smooth logarithmic integral), the detail to the first correction term, and the texture to the O(√x log x) oscillatory term that encodes where the Riemann zeros actually fall.

### 5.5 Shannon Capacity

Prime sequences are maximally entropic among deterministic sequences. The Riemann Hypothesis is equivalent to primes deviating from their smooth approximation as little as possible. PE based on integer frequencies therefore operates near Shannon channel capacity for the positional information channel. Geometric PE with log-uniform spacing operates below capacity due to redundant coverage.

### 5.6 The Unified Theory: Eight Views of One Structure

Walsh-Hadamard, LLL lattice reduction, spinor traversal, half-Möbius prediction, zero-crossing reconstruction, the n-ball construction, the Redheffer matrix, and RoPE rotation are not separate tools — they are the same operation expressed in different mathematical languages:

Walsh reads the multiplicative structure in Z/2Z. LLL finds irreducible (prime) vectors in a composite lattice. Zero-crossings detect where the Möbius function changes sign. Half-Möbius predicts where those boundaries are from topology alone. Spinor reads from both directions (the self-inverse property in traversal form). The n-ball reads it geometrically. The Redheffer matrix reads it arithmetically: R(i,j) = 1 if i divides j; invert it and the Möbius function falls out. RoPE reads it in the frequency domain.

The common operation: the multiplicative structure of the integers is self-inverse, and you can read it from either direction without computation. This is the theoretical foundation for the companion paper's claim that the KV cache is a view, not data.

### 5.7 Independent Confirmation: The K-corr → PPL Scaling Law

If K vectors encode position through the multiplicative lattice, then reconstruction errors in K should propagate to output errors in a specific, predictable way: quadratically (from the bilinear K·Q·V structure of attention) and modulated by the model's capacity to average over many heads.

We measured perplexity degradation versus K reconstruction fidelity across 9 configurations spanning 3 (model, quantization) pairs — Dolphin 1B Q8, Qwen3-8B Q8, Qwen3-8B Q3 — and 4 orders of magnitude of PPL ratio (baseline to catastrophic). The data fits a simple law within ±20%:

    log(PPL / PPL_base) ≈ 4700 · (1 − K_corr)² / (params^1.1 · bits^1.5)

The three exponents have direct interpretations consistent with the lattice theory:

**Quadratic in (1 − K_corr).** Attention scores are bilinear in K·Q^T, so small angular errors in K vectors compound through the softmax. The observed quadratic is exactly what theory predicts: if K error projects onto a random direction in a d-dimensional space, the expected attention-score error is O(ε²) where ε is the K-corr deficit.

**Sub-linear in params (exponent 1.1).** Larger models have more heads and higher effective dimension, so uncorrelated K errors average out more effectively. The 1.1 exponent is close to inverse-linear — consistent with averaging over heads scaling roughly as N_heads, which scales roughly linearly with parameter count in well-designed architectures.

**Super-linear in weight precision (exponent 1.5).** Low-precision W_Q/W_K matrices quantize the attention score directly; small K errors that Q8 rounds cleanly get amplified by Q4 or Q3 into measurable logit divergence. Precision amplifies error nonlinearly.

The scaling law is not itself a positional encoding result. But it provides quantitative, falsifiable confirmation of the central claim: K vectors are the channel through which position reaches attention, and the error structure of that channel is exactly what the lattice theory predicts.

## 6. Discussion

### 6.1 Dissolving the False Tradeoff

The RoPE/ALiBi tradeoff arose from encoding position as distance. Once position is reframed as arithmetic identity, relative position invariance (arithmetic relationships are translation-invariant in the lattice) and long-context stability (from harmonic resonance decay) emerge simultaneously.

### 6.2 Primes as Generators, Not Destinations

The falsification results show primes are the minimal generators but composites work equally well. This is a stronger result than "primes are special" — the entire multiplicative structure of the integers is the natural basis, and primes are simply the most economical way to span it.

### 6.3 The freq_scale Discovery

The learnable per-head freq_scale parameter in SpectralALiBi converges to values that reveal which tiers the model weights most heavily. This parameter allows the model to discover its natural harmonic basis from data — geometric RoPE's hardcoded base-10000 denies the model this degree of freedom.

### 6.4 Mechanistic Validation: The Spinor Sheet Bit

Section 5.4 predicted that outer-torus dimensions (Z/7Z, Z/11Z) would be fragile at the causal-mask boundary — the "open spiral" where phase wraps get truncated. A direct test: if those dimensions carry systematic sign errors that a single bit can correct, then a Möbius-inversion-based predictor extended with a 1-bit sheet flag (SU(2) double cover) should recover measurable compression.

Testing on Qwen3-8B Q8 hd=128 (Wiki.test, wiki-2 fixture):

| Configuration | PPL | K corr | Compression |
|---|---|---|---|
| MOBIUS default 5/5/5/5/5 | 7.31 | 0.999 | 2.6× |
| K+μ+3bit residual (no spinor) 5/5/5/5/5 | 7.70 | 0.980 | 2.6× |
| K+μ+3bit+spinor 5/4/4/4/5 | 7.30 | 0.988 | 2.8× |
| K+μ+3bit+spinor 3/3/3/3/3 | 7.32 | 0.972 | 3.3× |

The spinor path matches MOBIUS default quality at +27% compression. Without the sheet bit, the same predictor is 5% worse than MOBIUS at equal compression; with it, the gap closes and then exceeds the default.

The effect scales with head_dim as the theory predicts: on hd=64 (Dolphin 1B) the improvement is small (~4%) and within measurement noise; on hd=128 the squarefree basis accommodates ~2.3× more non-squarefree positions and the spinor correction accumulates into a clear Pareto shift. Causal-mask phase shredding is a real, measurable phenomenon in production models, and it is correctable with the exact topological structure the theory predicts.

### 6.5 Measurement Noise

GPU reductions on fp16 Vulkan (RTX 2060) are not bit-reproducible. Measurement noise on 1-chunk wiki.test in the 12-20 PPL regime is ±5 PPL. Tight ladder comparisons (differences < 0.5 PPL) are at or below this noise floor. Real effects of the scale reported here (5-7 PPL gap between lattice and non-lattice clusters in §4.3; 29× and 2.1× drops in the residual-bit saturation curve; basis switches that cross order-of-magnitude PPL boundaries) are well above noise. For methodological robustness, runs can be repeated and median-reported, or chunks ≥ 4 used to average. This paper's headline numbers come from the 20,000-step main experiment, where the noise floor is much lower than at 1-chunk fixture scale.

### 6.6 Limitations and Future Work

Scale: the main result is 300M params. 1B+ experiments are needed. Modern baselines (YaRN, NTK-aware RoPE, CARoPE) are not compared. Results are single-seed. The prediction that trained W_Q/W_K matrices converge toward the LLL-reduced basis of the prime lattice remains unverified. The combined effect of lattice PE frequencies + VHT2 spectral compression (tested independently) is the key integration experiment.

## 7. Conclusion

The RoPE/ALiBi tradeoff is not fundamental. It is an artifact of encoding position as distance rather than arithmetic identity. SpectralRoPEALiBi achieves relative position invariance, long-context stability, and arithmetic positional identity simultaneously — beating ALiBi at every context length.

The falsification suite provides the key insight: the active ingredient is the multiplicative lattice of the integers, not primality. Both primes and composites work because they encode the same structure. What fails is any encoding that discards it.

The ZetaZeroPredictor provides the deepest evidence: across two independent runs, geometric PE finds no stable solution while lattice-aligned PE achieves stable attractors. The multiplicative lattice is the natural spectral basis for the arithmetic structure that underlies both prime distribution and language.

The K-corr → PPL scaling law provides quantitative confirmation: K vectors are the channel through which position reaches attention, and the error structure of that channel is exactly quadratic-bilinear as the lattice theory predicts. The spinor sheet bit provides mechanistic confirmation: the causal-mask phase shredding predicted by the topological depth hierarchy is real, measurable, and correctable with the exact topological structure (SU(2) double cover) the theory specifies.

The geometric formalization — each prime as a torus dimension, with topological depth measured empirically from Z/3Z at 100% universality down to Z/7Z at 35% — connects positional encoding to the mathematics of n-ball constructions, Archimedes projections, and the Redheffer matrix. These are not analogies. They are different readings of the same self-inverse structure.

The universe encodes position in the arithmetic of the integers. So should we.
